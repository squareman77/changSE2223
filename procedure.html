Procedure

Step 1
All participants were first exposed to either one of two types of media, or none at all. No Media Group participants were not exposed to any media, and were given a questionnaire directly. Random Media Group participants were first exposed to a video titled “How It’s Made: Erasers.” Related Media Group participants were first exposed to a video titled “POWERFUL VIDEO: Why We Need to Stop Plastic Pollution in Our Oceans FOR GOOD | Oceana” published on YouTube by Oceana.
Step 2
One minute after viewing, the participants were asked to fill out the first of two questionnaires. The sole purpose of this questionnaire was to make participants conscious about their attitudes towards the environmental issue in question, plastic waste. It was intended that the questionnaire also seeked to make participants reflect on any contradictory attitudes and actions they might have.
Step 3
Each participant was asked to complete a second questionnaire one minute after completing the first questionnaire, which served as a measurement of cognitive dissonance. At this point, it was intended that participants had already been confronted with any of their hypocritical or contradictory behaviors, causing participants to have varying levels of cognitive dissonance. This questionnaire measured several known phenomena, behaviors, and attitudes associated with higher levels of cognitive dissonance on a numerical scale.
Step 4
The indices of the participants in each group were then statistically analyzed to determine a possible correlation between the media participants were exposed to, and the levels of cognitive dissonance observed.
